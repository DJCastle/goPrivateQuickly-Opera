Welcome to Go Private Quickly

One click opens a new private/incognito window — with an optional hardened mode that tightens privacy settings for that session.

Private-mode access

Browsers keep extensions switched off in private/incognito windows by default — it's a privacy safeguard, so add-ons can't quietly watch your private browsing. Go Private Quickly's whole job is to open and harden that private window, so it needs your go-ahead to work there. It's a one-time switch, and GPQ still collects nothing — no tracking, no data, no network requests.

Quick start

  • Click the GPQ icon in the toolbar, then choose Standard or Hardened.
  • Hardened tightens privacy settings for that session only.
  • Advanced options live in Settings.

What private mode does & doesn't do

  • It does stop this browser from saving your history, cookies, and form entries once the private window closes.
  • It doesn't make you anonymous online. Websites you visit, your internet provider, your employer, or your network administrator may still see your activity.
  • Want network privacy too? Connect a trusted VPN before browsing. GPQ never inspects, verifies, or controls VPN software — that's just a suggestion.
  • Files you download during private browsing stay on your computer after the window closes.
